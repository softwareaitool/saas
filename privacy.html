Privacy Policy

This is a general privacy policy template for a software service website. It explains common practices in a GDPR-friendly manner and should be tailored to your actual data flows and tools.

1. What this policy covers

This policy explains how WorkerFlow handles personal data when you visit the website and submit information through forms. If you add backend services, analytics, or integrations, you must update this policy to match your implementation.

2. Data we may collect

Depending on your usage, we may collect:

  • Contact details you submit (such as name, email, message)
  • Technical data (such as device type, browser, and approximate timestamps)
  • Cookies or local storage used for essential site behavior (if implemented)

3. How we use data

We use data to respond to inquiries, operate and secure the website, and improve content. We aim to collect only what is necessary for these purposes.

4. Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device. This template does not require non-essential cookies by default. If you add analytics or marketing cookies, you should provide a consent mechanism and describe cookie categories clearly.

5. Legal bases (GDPR)

Where GDPR applies, processing may be based on consent (for optional features), legitimate interests (site security and improvement), or contractual necessity (to provide requested services). Choose and document the correct basis for each data type you process.

6. Data retention

Keep data only as long as needed for the purpose it was collected. Set a retention period for contact requests and remove data when it is no longer required.

7. Sharing and transfers

We do not sell personal data. If you add service providers (such as hosting, email delivery, or support tools), you may share necessary data with them to operate the service. If data is transferred internationally, ensure you use appropriate safeguards.

8. Security

We use reasonable measures to protect information, such as access controls and transport security when available. No internet transmission method is guaranteed to be fully secure, so you should avoid sending sensitive information through basic contact forms.

9. Your rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict processing of your personal data, as well as the right to object to certain processing. You may also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority.

10. Contact

For privacy questions, contact privacy@example.com.

Implementation note: If you add analytics, marketing pixels, or third-party embeds, update this policy and consider cookie consent.
